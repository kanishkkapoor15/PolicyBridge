# Companies Act 2014 (Ireland)

## Overview
The Companies Act 2014 is the principal legislation governing the formation, operation, and regulation of companies in Ireland. It consolidated and reformed over 50 years of Irish company law into a single Act — the longest piece of legislation in the history of the Irish State.

**Effective Date:** 1 June 2015
**Jurisdiction:** Ireland
**Enforced by:** Companies Registration Office (CRO), Office of the Director of Corporate Enforcement (ODCE — now the Corporate Enforcement Authority, CEA)

---

## Company Types

### Part 2 — Private Company Limited by Shares (LTD)
The default company type in Ireland. Key features:
- Can have 1 to 149 members
- Single-document constitution (no separate memorandum and articles of association)
- Can have a single director (must still have a separate company secretary)
- May pass majority written resolutions without a meeting

### Part 16 — Designated Activity Company (DAC)
A company with a stated objects clause limiting its activities. Used for regulated entities (banks, insurance companies) or joint ventures.

### Part 17 — Public Limited Company (PLC)
Can offer shares to the public. Minimum share capital of €25,000.

### Part 18 — Company Limited by Guarantee (CLG)
No share capital; members guarantee liabilities up to a stated amount. Used for charities, clubs, and non-profits.

## Directors' Duties (Part 5, Chapter 2)

### Section 228 — Fiduciary Duties
Directors have the following duties:

1. **Act in good faith** in what the director considers to be the interests of the company (Section 228(1)(a))
2. **Act honestly and responsibly** in relation to the conduct of the affairs of the company (Section 228(1)(b))
3. **Act in accordance with the company's constitution** and exercise powers only for lawful purposes (Section 228(1)(c))
4. **Not use the company's property, information, or opportunities** for the director's own or anyone else's benefit unless permitted by the constitution or approved by the company in general meeting (Section 228(1)(d))
5. **Not agree to restrict the director's power** to exercise independent judgement unless permitted by the constitution or by a pre-existing contract (Section 228(1)(e))
6. **Avoid conflicts of interest** between the director's duties and other interests, unless released by the company (Section 228(1)(f))
7. **Exercise care, skill, and diligence** — both the care, skill, and diligence that would be exercised by a reasonable person in the same position, and the care, skill, and diligence that may reasonably be expected given the director's own knowledge and experience (Section 228(1)(g))
8. **Have regard to the interests of the company's members** (Section 228(1)(h))

### Section 229 — Duty to Disclose Interests
Directors must disclose any interest in contracts or proposed contracts with the company.

## Company Secretary (Section 129)
- Every company must have a company secretary
- The directors are responsible for ensuring the company secretary has the skills or resources necessary to discharge their statutory and other duties
- The company secretary is responsible for maintaining statutory registers, filing annual returns, and ensuring compliance with the Companies Act

---

## Annual Return and Filing Obligations

### Section 343 — Annual Return
- Every company must file an **annual return** with the CRO within **56 days** of the annual return date
- The annual return must include financial statements (unless exempt as a small/micro company)
- Late filing results in **loss of audit exemption** for 2 years and late filing penalties

### Section 347 — Financial Statements
- Directors must prepare financial statements that give a **true and fair view** of the company's assets, liabilities, and financial position
- Statements must comply with **Irish GAAP (FRS 102)** or **IFRS** (for listed companies)
- Must include a directors' report discussing principal activities, business review, dividends, and events after the balance sheet date

---

## Corporate Governance Obligations

### Section 158 — Directors
- A private company (LTD) must have at least **1 director** (minimum 2 for DAC and PLC)
- At least **1 director must be resident** in an EEA state — or the company must hold a bond (Section 137) or have a certificate from the CRO confirming a real and continuous link with economic activity in Ireland
- Directors must be **natural persons** (no corporate directors, subject to limited exceptions)
- No person may be a director of more than **25 companies** at any one time (Section 142, subject to exceptions)

### Section 175 — Directors' Compliance Statement
- Medium and large companies must include a **directors' compliance statement** in the directors' report
- This must acknowledge directors' responsibility to secure the company's compliance with its relevant obligations (company law, tax law)
- Must confirm that appropriate arrangements and structures are in place (or explain any failures to comply)

### Section 167 — Loans to Directors
- A company may not make a loan to, or provide a guarantee or security in connection with a loan to, a director or a connected person unless:
  - The aggregate value does not exceed 10% of the company's net assets
  - The transaction is approved by the company in general meeting
  - Specific exemptions apply (e.g., small loans up to €5,000)

---

## Audit Requirements

### Section 333 — Statutory Audit
- All companies must appoint a statutory auditor unless they qualify for the **audit exemption**
- **Small company** audit exemption: turnover ≤ €12 million, balance sheet total ≤ €6 million, employees ≤ 50 (must meet 2 of 3 criteria)
- **Micro company** exemption: turnover ≤ €700,000, balance sheet total ≤ €350,000, employees ≤ 10

---

## Beneficial Ownership Register

### EU (Anti-Money Laundering: Beneficial Ownership of Corporate Entities) Regulations 2019
- All Irish companies must maintain a **register of beneficial owners** — individuals who hold more than 25% of shares, voting rights, or otherwise exercise control
- This information must be filed with the **Register of Beneficial Ownership of Companies and Industrial and Provident Societies (RBO)**
- Failure to file is a criminal offence

---

## Winding Up and Insolvency

### Voluntary Winding Up
- **Members' voluntary winding up** — where the company is solvent (directors make a declaration of solvency)
- **Creditors' voluntary winding up** — where the company is insolvent

### Section 570 — Reckless Trading
A director may be held personally liable for company debts if they were knowingly party to carrying on the company's business in a reckless manner.

### Section 610 — Restriction of Directors
The court may restrict a director of an insolvent company for 5 years from acting as a director or secretary unless the company meets minimum capital requirements (€500,000 for PLC, €100,000 for LTD).

---

## Key Compliance Points for Policy Migration
1. **Corporate governance policies** must reference Irish director duties under **Section 228** — not US fiduciary duty standards
2. **Board composition** must include at least **1 EEA-resident director** or have appropriate alternatives in place
3. **Directors' compliance statements** are required for medium/large companies
4. **Annual return obligations** to the CRO must replace US SEC or state filing references
5. **Company secretary** requirements must be addressed — this is a mandatory role in Ireland
6. **Beneficial ownership** register requirements must be included in corporate governance policies
7. **Loans to directors** restrictions under Section 167 must be reflected in company policies
8. **Audit committee** and financial reporting must reference Irish GAAP (FRS 102) or IFRS, not US GAAP
9. Remove references to US-specific corporate governance frameworks (Sarbanes-Oxley, Dodd-Frank) unless dual-listed
