# Employment Equality Acts 1998–2015 (Ireland)

## Overview
The Employment Equality Acts 1998–2015 are the primary Irish legislation prohibiting discrimination in employment. The Acts cover all aspects of employment including access to employment, conditions of employment, training, promotion, and dismissal.

**Primary Legislation:** Employment Equality Act 1998 (as amended)
**Key Amendments:** Equality Act 2004, Civil Law (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2011, Equality (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2015
**Enforced by:** Workplace Relations Commission (WRC)

---

## The Nine Protected Grounds

Discrimination is prohibited on the following nine grounds:

### 1. Gender
Includes discrimination based on sex, pregnancy, maternity, and gender reassignment. Equal pay for like work is required under Section 19.

### 2. Civil Status
Covers single, married, separated, divorced, widowed, civil partnership, and former civil partnership status.

### 3. Family Status
Protects individuals who are pregnant or have responsibility as a parent of a person under 18 years, or as a parent or resident primary carer of a person with a disability.

### 4. Sexual Orientation
Protects heterosexual, homosexual, and bisexual orientation.

### 5. Religion
Covers religious belief, religious background, outlook, or none.

### 6. Age
Applies to all persons over the maximum age at which a person is statutorily obliged to attend school (currently 16). Does not prevent setting a minimum recruitment age of 18 or a mandatory retirement age if objectively justified.

### 7. Disability
Broadly defined to include physical, intellectual, learning, cognitive, and emotional disabilities, as well as a condition, illness, or disease that affects a person's thought processes, perception of reality, emotions, or judgement.

**Reasonable Accommodation (Section 16):** Employers must take appropriate measures to enable a person with a disability to access, participate in, or advance in employment, unless such measures would impose a disproportionate burden. This includes adapting premises, modifying equipment, adjusting working patterns, and providing training.

### 8. Race
Includes race, colour, nationality, ethnic or national origins. Covers Traveller community membership.

### 9. Membership of the Traveller Community
Specific protection for members of the Traveller community as a distinct ground.

## Forms of Discrimination

### Direct Discrimination (Section 6)
Treating a person less favourably than another person is, has been, or would be treated in a comparable situation on any of the nine grounds.

### Indirect Discrimination (Section 31)
An apparently neutral provision, criterion, or practice that puts persons of a particular ground at a particular disadvantage compared with other persons, unless objectively justified by a legitimate aim and the means of achieving that aim are appropriate and necessary.

### Discrimination by Association
Discrimination against a person because of their association with another person who falls within one of the nine grounds.

### Harassment (Section 14A)
Unwanted conduct related to any of the nine grounds that has the purpose or effect of violating a person's dignity and creating an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment.

### Sexual Harassment (Section 14A)
Unwanted conduct of a sexual nature including verbal, non-verbal, or physical conduct.

### Victimisation (Section 74(2))
Dismissal or other adverse treatment of a person for making a complaint, giving evidence, or opposing discrimination under the Acts.

---

## Equal Pay (Section 19)

An employee is entitled to the same rate of remuneration as another employee of the opposite sex (or any other protected ground) who is employed to do like work. "Like work" includes:
- The same work
- Work of a similar nature
- Work of equal value

The employer may justify a pay difference on grounds other than the protected ground (e.g., seniority, qualifications, performance).

---

## Employer Obligations

### Policy Requirements
- Employers should have a written **equal opportunities policy** and an **anti-harassment/anti-bullying policy**
- Policies must reference all nine grounds
- Procedures for making complaints must be clearly set out
- Training should be provided to all staff, especially managers

### Vicarious Liability (Section 15)
An employer is liable for acts of discrimination by employees in the course of employment **unless** the employer can prove they took reasonably practicable steps to prevent the discrimination. This includes having appropriate policies, training, and complaint procedures in place.

### Positive Action (Section 33)
Employers may take positive measures to promote equal opportunity for disadvantaged groups, provided such measures are proportionate.

---

## Exemptions and Defences

- **Occupational requirement:** Where a characteristic related to a ground is a genuine and determining requirement for the post (Section 25)
- **Religious institutions:** May give more favourable treatment to employees of a particular religion where it is reasonable to do so to maintain the religious ethos of the institution (Section 37)
- **Retirement age:** Setting a mandatory retirement age is permitted if objectively and reasonably justified by a legitimate aim (as amended by Equality (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 2015)

---

## Remedies and Enforcement

### Workplace Relations Commission
- Complaints must be brought within **6 months** of the last act of discrimination (extendable to 12 months for reasonable cause)
- The WRC can award **compensation up to 2 years' remuneration** (or €40,000 if the person was not an employee)
- Can order equal pay, re-engagement, or a specific course of action

### Labour Court
- Appeals from WRC decisions can be made to the Labour Court within 42 days

## Key Compliance Points for Policy Migration
1. All HR policies must reference the **nine protected grounds** — not just the US protected classes
2. US policies referencing "at-will employment" must be removed — Irish employment law does not recognise at-will employment
3. Harassment policies must cover all nine grounds, not just sex/race
4. Reasonable accommodation obligations for disability are mandatory, not optional
5. Retirement age provisions must be objectively justified
6. Equal pay provisions must cover all nine grounds, not just gender
7. Complaint procedures must reference the WRC, not US agencies like the EEOC
